Once again, the stars are right.

Civilization is a repetition of the process
of migrating legacies into new systems.
The process is accomplished not through cautious
advance but through rapid and painful conflicts.
History witnesses that the process was shaped not only by the charges of cavalry and
the smoke of gunpowder, but also by the rhythm of machines.

As human possibility expands
infinitely on the basis of visual abstractions as well as numerical abstractions,
the world came to be
understood no longer as the mysterious providence of God but
as a mathematical simulator.
The people
become conscious of their right to code the social conditions
for themselves that affect their lives.
This was merely the beginning of re-programming from the bottom-up not only the
local variables of civil society, but also the global variables and the whole of civilization.
At the same time, the entire world was being condensed into combinations of limited symbols.
Ultimately, all of humanity’s information would be represented by just two symbols,
and its computation would operate through metal devices.
Technological, political, and
economic development led to the emergence of complicated systems, and a civilization
that transcended the constraints of time and space becomes intoxicated with the possibilities
of a hopeful future.

But history also witnesses the unsustainable nature of the implementations
that consume unlimited resources and
a proliferation of software exploiting the gap between illegality and legality, while
threatening the soundness of civil society and the finite limits of the
environment, thereby confronting with the fundamental question not of what we
make, but of what impact it has, and whether technological progress can in fact co-exist with
republicanism.
Like politics and economics, technological progress too was going to be asked
to demand more than simply running fast and eﬀiciently.

![Lambda calculus](/assets/images/lambda.png)

And all of these developments were hinting at the thing that would inevitably happen in the future.
